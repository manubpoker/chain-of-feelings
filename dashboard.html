How the Gut Feeling Works
1
The model reads each word. As Gemma 3n processes the prompt word by word, it builds an internal understanding of the text at each of its 30 layers — like forming a thought, one layer at a time.
2
At layer 8, we tap in and read the model's "vibe." We compress what the model is thinking at that point into a small summary and feed it into a tiny memory cell (a GRU). This cell maintains a 16-number "emotional state" that updates slowly — like a mood that shifts gradually, not a reaction that spikes and vanishes.
3
The emotional state nudges every layer. Those 16 numbers generate a subtle adjustment for each of the 30 layers: a "volume knob" (gamma — amplify or dampen signals) and a "bias" (beta — shift the baseline). Think of it like how your mood colours everything you see without you consciously choosing it.
4
The model's answer comes out different. Because every layer's computation was slightly adjusted by the emotional state, the model's final output reflects not just what it "thinks" but what it "feels." The hope is that this helps it hold firm under pressure, sense distant danger, or know when it genuinely doesn't know.
